# Example: Opening Scene Critique

## Original Passage (Telling-heavy)

John was nervous as he entered the room. He saw that everyone was looking at him.
He felt his heart beating fast and realized he was sweating. The room was very big
and had many people in it. He thought about turning around and leaving, but he knew
he had to give his speech. He walked to the podium and began to speak.

## Revised Passage (Showing)

The door swung shut behind John with a click that sounded too final. Two hundred
faces turned toward him. Two hundred sets of expectations. He wiped his palms
against his trousers — once, twice — but the fabric came away damp. The podium
waited at the far end of the ballroom, and every step toward it felt like wading
deeper into water. Halfway there, his feet nearly betrayed him, angling back toward
the exit. *No.* He set his jaw and kept walking. At the podium, he gripped the
edges until his knuckles ached, then leaned into the microphone.

"Thank you for having me."

His voice didn't crack. That was something.

## What Changed

1. Filter words removed ("saw," "felt," "realized," "thought," "knew")
2. Emotion shown through physical action (wiping palms, gripping podium, jaw setting)
3. Sensory details added (the click of the door, the damp fabric)
4. Interior thought integrated naturally ("No." in free indirect discourse)
5. Specific details over generic ("Two hundred faces" instead of "many people")
6. Metaphor used meaningfully ("wading deeper into water")
7. Ending with a small, specific victory that reveals character
