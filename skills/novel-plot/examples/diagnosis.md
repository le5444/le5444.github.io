# Example: Plot Structure Diagnosis

## Scenario: "My middle act is sagging"

A writer brings you a fantasy novel draft. The protagonist, Kira, must deliver
a magical artifact across a war-torn kingdom to prevent an ancient evil from
awakening. The journey should feel urgent, but the middle act (chapters 12-28)
feels slow.

## Diagnosis Questions

1. **What are the obstacles?** Kira faces bandits (ch. 14), a river crossing (ch. 17),
   a suspicious village (ch. 20), another bandit attack (ch. 23). The obstacles are
   repetitive — all are external travel hazards.

2. **What's at stake in each chapter?** In most mid-act chapters, Kira just needs to
   "keep moving." There's no escalation of what she personally stands to lose.

3. **Where's the internal conflict?** Kira's backstory reveals she once failed to
   protect someone — but this never comes up during the journey.

4. **Does each obstacle teach her something?** Not really. She's the same person
   after the bandits and after the river.

## Recommendations

1. **Vary obstacle types**: Add moral dilemmas (a village asks for her help, but
   stopping costs time), interpersonal conflict (her guide has a different plan),
   and internal obstacles (her past failure makes her freeze at a critical moment).

2. **Escalate personal stakes**: Make the obstacles attack her specific fear of
   failing to protect. The bandits capture her guide. She must risk the mission
   to save them — confronting her trauma.

3. **Midpoint revision**: At the midpoint, reveal that the artifact she carries
   is slowly corrupting her. Now every step toward the destination is also a step
   toward losing herself. The external journey becomes an internal one.

4. **Chain obstacles causally**: The village she helps (or refuses to help) tips
   off the antagonist to her location, leading to the next obstacle. Nothing
   should be random.

## Revised Middle Act Outline

| Chapter | Beat | External | Internal |
|---------|------|----------|----------|
| 12 | Rising Action | Enter disputed territory | Kira's guide mentions a past failure |
| 14 | Obstacle | Village asks for help with raiders | Kira faces her fear of failing others |
| 17 | Consequence | Helping the village delays them; enemy gains ground | Guilt: she helped one village but may doom everyone |
| 20 | Midpoint | Discover artifact's corrupting effect | She's becoming the threat she's trying to stop |
| 23 | Escalation | Guide is captured; Kira must choose | Confronts her past failure directly |
| 26 | Dark Night | Rescue fails; guide dies | "I can't protect anyone" — her fear confirmed |
| 28 | Rally | Finds guide's journal with a hidden plan | "Protecting isn't about never failing — it's about trying anyway" |
