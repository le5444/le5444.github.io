# Example: Character Creation Session

## Writer's Request
"I need a protagonist for a cyberpunk thriller. She's a hacker who discovers
a corporate conspiracy. But I want her to feel real, not like a cliché."

## Character Development

### Surface Identity
- **Name**: Mira Chen
- **Age**: 27
- **Occupation**: Former corporate security architect, now freelance data recovery specialist (legal cover for less legal work)
- **Appearance**: Deliberately forgettable — average height, muted clothing, hair that can be styled up or down depending on what ID she's using. Has a burn scar on her left forearm from an accident she doesn't discuss.

### Voice & Mannerisms
- Speaks in short, efficient sentences. Doesn't waste words — or trust.
- Fidgets with a data chip on a chain around her neck, especially when anxious
- Types faster than she talks; more comfortable in code than conversation
- Dry, dark humor that catches people off guard

### Backstory
- Raised in a corporate arcology by parents who were mid-level execs — she grew up seeing how the system works from the inside
- At 16, she discovered a flaw in the arcology's life support AI that could have killed thousands. She reported it anonymously. The corporation buried the report.
- She tried to go public. Her parents were "reassigned." She hasn't seen them in eleven years.
- That's when she learned: the system isn't broken. It's working exactly as designed.

### Inner World
- **False Belief**: "The system can't be beaten. The best you can do is survive within it."
- **Fear**: Becoming like the people who destroyed her family — using power to hurt others
- **Desire**: To disappear completely, to owe nothing to anyone
- **Need**: To believe that fighting back matters, that some things are worth the risk
- **Contradiction**: She insists she doesn't care about anyone, but she's never turned down a client who genuinely needed help

### Arc Trajectory
1. **Start**: Mira takes only safe jobs, never targets the corporations that ruined her
2. **Inciting Incident**: A whistleblower's data falls into her hands — proof of a conspiracy that's killing people
3. **Early Refusal**: She tries to pawn the data off to someone else; the contact is murdered
4. **Rising Arc**: She's forced to protect people: a journalist, a witness's child, eventually a whole community
5. **Dark Night**: She has a chance to trade the data for information about her parents — she almost takes it
6. **Climax Choice**: She chooses to burn the leverage that could reunite her family, because releasing the data will save thousands
7. **Transformation**: She stops surviving and starts fighting. She's no longer invisible.

### What Makes Her Not a Cliché
- She's not a loner by choice — she's a loner because she's afraid of losing people again
- She's not "the best hacker in the world" — she's very good at one specific thing (corporate security systems she helped build)
- Her arc isn't about learning to trust others — it's about learning that her actions matter even when she can't control the outcome
- Her humor isn't quippy one-liners — it's the quiet, bitter observations of someone who's been paying attention
