# Revision Checklists

## Developmental Edit: Full Manuscript Diagnostic

### Story Structure
- [ ] Does the story have a clear beginning, middle, and end?
- [ ] Does the opening hook grab attention within the first 2 pages?
- [ ] Is the inciting incident at the right point (roughly 10-15%)?
- [ ] Does the first plot point (25%) mark a clear "no turning back" moment?
- [ ] Does the midpoint (50%) meaningfully raise the stakes?
- [ ] Is there a genuine "All Is Lost" moment (75%)?
- [ ] Does the climax (88-95%) deliver on the story's promises?
- [ ] Is the denouement satisfying without overstaying its welcome?

### Plot
- [ ] Does every scene have a cause-and-effect relationship with the next?
- [ ] Are there plot holes or logical inconsistencies?
- [ ] Do subplots connect to and enrich the main plot?
- [ ] Are all planted clues/payoffs tracked and resolved?
- [ ] Does the protagonist drive the plot through their choices?
- [ ] Are there scenes that could be cut without affecting the story?

### Character
- [ ] Does the protagonist have a clear arc (or a clear reason for a flat arc)?
- [ ] Is the protagonist active (making choices) rather than reactive?
- [ ] Does the protagonist have both strengths and flaws?
- [ ] Does the antagonist have believable motivations?
- [ ] Are supporting characters distinct and necessary?
- [ ] Do character relationships evolve throughout the story?

### Pacing
- [ ] Does tension escalate overall (with strategic valleys)?
- [ ] Are there chapters where nothing significant happens?
- [ ] Are major plot beats given enough space to land emotionally?
- [ ] Is the ending neither rushed nor drawn out?
- [ ] Do chapter endings compel continued reading?

### Theme
- [ ] Is the story's central theme clear (even if never stated explicitly)?
- [ ] Do character arcs reinforce the theme?
- [ ] Does the antagonist represent thematic opposition?
- [ ] Are there scenes that explore the theme from different angles?

## Scene-Level Edit Checklist

### For Each Scene, Ask:
- [ ] What does the POV character want in this scene?
- [ ] What obstacle(s) prevent them from getting it?
- [ ] What changes by the end of this scene?
- [ ] Why does this scene need to exist? (What would be lost by cutting it?)
- [ ] Is the POV consistent throughout?
- [ ] Does the scene start as late as possible and end as early as possible?
- [ ] Does the scene's length match its importance?
- [ ] Is there a balance of action, dialogue, and interiority?

### Scene Transitions
- [ ] Do scenes flow naturally from one to the next?
- [ ] Are time jumps clearly signaled?
- [ ] Do POV shifts occur at chapter/scene breaks (not mid-scene)?
- [ ] Does each scene leave the reader wanting to continue?

## Line Edit Checklist

### Prose
- [ ] Filter words removed where possible (saw, heard, felt, realized, etc.)
- [ ] Weak verbs replaced with stronger alternatives
- [ ] Adverbs replaced with stronger verbs where possible
- [ ] Redundant phrasing eliminated
- [ ] Passive voice converted to active where appropriate
- [ ] Sentence variety: mix of short, medium, and long sentences
- [ ] No consecutive sentences starting the same way

### Dialogue
- [ ] Each character has a distinct voice
- [ ] Dialogue tags are predominantly "said" or action beats
- [ ] Dialogue serves multiple purposes (reveal character + advance plot + create tension)
- [ ] Characters don't say exactly what they mean (subtext)
- [ ] "As you know, Bob" exposition through dialogue eliminated
- [ ] Dialogue sounds natural when read aloud

### Description
- [ ] Sensory details engage at least 2-3 senses in important descriptions
- [ ] Descriptions filtered through POV character's perspective
- [ ] Specific, concrete details over generic ones
- [ ] Setting integrated with action (not in isolated description blocks)
- [ ] No "white room syndrome" — locations are grounded in sensory detail

### Opening Chapter Special Check
- [ ] First page hooks reader (curiosity, voice, tension, or wonder)
- [ ] Backstory limited to essential details only
- [ ] Protagonist's want and problem established
- [ ] Tone and genre signaled clearly
- [ ] World/setting established through action, not exposition dump

## Common Prose Problems & Fixes

| Problem | Example | Fix |
|---------|---------|-----|
| Filtering | "She saw the door open" | "The door opened" |
| Telling emotion | "He was angry" | "His jaw clenched. The pen snapped in his grip." |
| Weak verb + adverb | "walked slowly" | "trudged" / "ambled" / "drifted" |
| Over-explaining | "She was sad because her dog died" | Trust the reader; show the empty leash, the untouched food bowl |
| Cliché | "cold as ice" | Find a fresh comparison specific to your character's world |
| Passive voice | "The ball was thrown by John" | "John threw the ball" |
| Nominalization | "She made a decision" | "She decided" |
| Redundant modifiers | "whispered softly" | "whispered" |
| Distancing language | "She began to walk" | "She walked" |
